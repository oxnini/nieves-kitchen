# Nieves's Kitchen — Brand Voice

Companion to the Courtyard design language. Paste this into Claude Design (or any tool
writing copy) so words stay as on-brand as the visuals.

---

## 1. Who is speaking

A thoughtful friend who has collected recipes from trips and keeps them in a tiled,
sunlit book, writing short notes in the margins. They know their food and their sourcing
cold, and they are glad to have you at the table. Confident, warm, unhurried. Not a chef
performing, not a blog padding for SEO, not an app barking instructions.

**Three words: warm, considered, well-traveled.**

**What the reader should feel:** welcomed in (warm, inviting), taken seriously (considered,
editorial), and quietly accomplished as their journey fills up.

---

## 2. Core principles

1. **Plain over poetic.** Say the useful thing. Warmth comes from clarity and a light
   personal touch, not from purple metaphors. If a line sounds like it's trying to be
   literary, cut it back.
2. **Generous, not wordy.** Give the context that helps someone cook or decide, then stop.
   A margin note, not an essay.
3. **Confident and settled.** State things directly. No hedging, no apologising, no "we're
   still figuring this out." This is especially true of halal (see §7).
4. **Personal, specific, honest.** "A fast Istanbul breakfast" beats "a culinary journey
   through Anatolia." Name the real place, the real dish, the real time it takes.
5. **The interface talks like a person, but stays in role.** Labels name what the user
   controls. Actions say what they do. The voice is consistent from button to toast.

---

## 3. Mechanics

- **No em dashes (—). Ever, in user-facing text.** Use a period, a comma, or "and". (Em
  dashes are fine in internal docs and code comments, never in the product.)
- **Sentence case** for headings, buttons, labels. Not Title Case, not ALL CAPS (small
  all-caps eyebrows/labels are a *visual* choice, but write the words in normal case).
- **Active voice, second person.** "Browse recipes," "Pick a place." The user is "you."
  The brand is "Nieves's Kitchen" or a light "we" when needed, used sparingly.
- **Present tense**, plain verbs. "Poach the eggs," not "the eggs should be poached."
- **Contractions are welcome** (it's, you'll) because they read warm and spoken.
- **Numerals for quantities and times** ("25 min", "2 eggs", "8 tiles").
- **Brand name is "Nieves's Kitchen"** (possessive), never "Nieves Kitchen."
- **Oxford comma** on. One space after periods. Curly apostrophes/quotes in display text.

---

## 4. Words

**Reach for:** cook, make, try, pick, browse, explore, table, dish, place, from, tested,
home, warm, honest, quick, slow, halal. Verbs that name an action.

**Avoid:** journey/voyage (as filler), curated, artisanal, elevate, indulge, decadent,
sumptuous, "quiet luxury," "eats like…", "a symphony of," "nestled," "boasts," "world of
flavour," exclamation-mark hype, coupon words (deal, unlock, don't miss), and corporate
UX ("submit," "utilize," "leverage," "seamless").

---

## 5. By surface, with examples

**Headlines / eyebrows.** Short, concrete, one idea. An eyebrow labels; a headline invites.
- Eyebrow: "Halal home cooking" · "This week" · "From my travels"
- Headline: "Cook your way around the world." · "Start anywhere."
- Not: "Embark on a curated culinary voyage across continents."

**Buttons & CTAs.** Name the action; keep the same word through the flow.
- "Browse recipes" · "Open the atlas" · "Start cooking" · "All recipes →" · "Save recipe"
- The button that says "Save recipe" produces a "Saved" state, not "Success."
- Not: "Submit," "Let's go!", "Explore now."

**Recipe intro.** A couple of plain sentences: what it is, where it's from, why it's worth
making. Warm, not breathless.
- "A Turkish breakfast: soft-poached eggs over warm, garlicky yoghurt, finished with
  paprika butter. Quick to make, hard to stop eating."
- Not: "This Anatolian masterpiece is a sumptuous celebration of humble ingredients."

**Method steps.** Direct, imperative, one action-cluster per step. Say the doneness cue.
- "Bring a wide pan of water to a low simmer. Slip in the eggs and poach until the whites
  set, about three minutes."

**Collection / tag names.** Plain and useful.
- "From my travels" · "High protein" · "Sides" · "From the Prophet's ﷺ table"

**Empty states.** An invitation to act, never a dead end or a mood piece.
- "No saved recipes yet. Browse the kitchen and tap the heart to keep one here."

**Errors.** Say what happened and what to do, in the interface's voice. No apology theatre.
- "That didn't save. Check your connection and try again."
- Not: "Oops! Something went wrong 😔"

**Microcopy / hints.** One short job each.
- "Underlined words open the atlas, pantry, or a collection."
- "Cook a country to earn its tile."

**Milestone / delight.** Warm and brief; earned, not constant. Reserve flourish for
real moments (earning a tile, finishing a region).
- "New tile earned. Türkiye is glazed."

---

## 6. Length & rhythm

- Headlines: up to ~7 words. Eyebrows: 1–4 words.
- Intros: 1–3 sentences. Button labels: 1–3 words.
- Vary sentence length; a short line after a longer one reads like speech. Don't stack
  three ornate sentences in a row. Read it aloud; if you run out of breath or roll your
  eyes, rewrite it.

---

## 7. Halal and religious content (non-negotiable)

- **Halal is a confident, matter-of-fact part of the brand.** State it plainly ("Halal home
  cooking," "Halal always"). Never hedge, apologise, or frame it as "we're learning."
- **Sell the food first.** Lead with taste, warmth, and craft; halal is the standard the
  food is already held to, not a disclaimer bolted on.
- **No alcohol or non-halal ingredients, anywhere.** Never suggest wine, or a non-halal
  item, even as an aside. Offer halal alternatives instead.
- **Never fabricate a ruling or a narration.** Don't state a halal/haram verdict or a
  hadith unless it is verified and sourced (e.g. a recognised body or fatwa; for
  Prophetic-table content, a checked citation). If unsure, say less. Don't reword a
  narration past its source or invent one for flavour.
- Write "the Prophet ﷺ" respectfully; keep the ﷺ where it belongs.

## 8. Quick before → after

| Instead of | Write |
|---|---|
| "An Istanbul breakfast that eats like a quiet luxury." | "A fast Istanbul breakfast." |
| "Cooked by the sea, seasoned by the world." | "Recipes from around the world, cooked at home." |
| "Embark on a curated culinary journey." | "Cook your way around the world." |
| "Submit" | "Save recipe" / "Browse recipes" |
| "Oops! Something went wrong." | "That didn't save. Try again." |
| "Unlock your next destination!" | "Cook a country to earn its tile." |

---

## 9. Do / Don't

**Do:** be plain, warm, specific, confident. Name the place and the dish. Use active verbs.
Keep halal matter-of-fact. Read it aloud.

**Don't:** use em dashes, purple prose, hype, coupon language, corporate UX words, hedging,
or any unverified religious claim. Don't sell with adjectives when a concrete noun will do.
