# GPT-image-1 Prompts — Nieves Kitchen Tier Badges

Five badges, generated as separate 1024×1024 transparent PNGs. Use the **shared system block** at the top of every prompt to lock the brand DNA, then append one **tier-specific block** to swap in the unique artifact, ornamental ring, and material treatment.

A combined **lineup-sheet prompt** is provided at the bottom for first-pass system validation (generates all five in one image so you can judge consistency before committing to individual passes).

> **Model settings.** `gpt-image-1` (or "GPT-image 2" / image-2 in newer naming). `quality: "high"`, `size: "1024x1024"`, `background: "transparent"`. Do not use `style: "vivid"` — the warm-vintage palette wants the natural rendering.

---

## Shared system block (paste at top of every per-tier prompt)

```
Style anchors:
- Vintage letterpress and hand-drawn copperplate-etching aesthetic, in the visual family of an early-20th-century European passport stamp or a hand-set cookbook frontispiece. Linework reads as drawn with a fine nib, then ink-stamped onto warm cream paper — slight ink bleed and distress at the edges of every stroke, no glossy digital finishes, no gradients, no drop shadows, no 3D rendering, no airbrushing.
- Composition is a circular badge, centered, filling the frame with a small margin of negative space.
- Color palette is strictly limited to: a warm dark brown ink (roughly #5C3A28), a warm terracotta accent (roughly #C2613A), a parchment cream paper tone (roughly #FDF6EC), and — only where this prompt explicitly calls for it — a vintage gold-leaf accent (roughly #B8893A with subtle gold-foil texture). No blues, no greens, no purples, no grays. The cream paper tone is the background; everything else is ink or foil pressed onto it.
- Background is transparent outside the badge, but the badge itself sits on its own small piece of warm cream paper that is part of the artwork (the badge is an artifact pressed onto paper, not floating in void).
- Typography uses a refined book-serif similar to Literata or Caslon. Letterforms are sharp where they're crisply printed and slightly broken or distressed where they're stamped or pressed. No sans-serif, no display fonts, no calligraphic scripts.

Fixed structural template — present in EVERY tier badge unless explicitly overridden:
- Top arch (along the upper inner curve of the badge): a Roman numeral in small serif caps.
- Dead center: a two-letter "NK" monogram in elegant book-serif caps. The two letters share a common baseline; "N" sits left, "K" sits right; they may share a hairline horizontal rule beneath them.
- Bottom arch (along the lower inner curve of the badge): the words "NIEVES' KITCHEN" in serif caps with letter-spacing, separated by a small bullet ornament on either side.
- Ornamental ring: the band between the top arch / bottom arch and the central monogram is filled with a tier-specific motif described below.

Negative prompts (do not include any of these): photorealism, glossy plastic finishes, neon colors, blue or green tones, cartoon styling, anime styling, modern flat-design vectors, generic medal-of-honor military iconography, religious crosses, currency-bill engraving of human faces, watermarks, signature marks, frame borders outside the badge itself, stock-photo lighting.
```

## Tier I — New Explorer (luggage tag)

```
Tier 1 of 5 — "New Explorer." This is the humblest, lightest badge in the set. It is a paper artifact, not yet ink-stamped.

Artifact: a round cream card-stock luggage tag, roughly 80% of frame width, with a reinforced metal eyelet centered at the top edge and a short loop of frayed twine threaded through it (the twine is small, decorative, and reads as paper craft, not photographic). The card-stock has one slightly torn lower edge with visible paper fibers. The card stock has a faint subtle paper grain.

Top arch: the Roman numeral "I" in small serif caps.
Center: the "NK" monogram, printed in clean brown ink.
Bottom arch: "NIEVES' KITCHEN" in serif caps with bullet ornaments.

Ornamental ring (between the bottom arch and the monogram): a simple compass rose — four cardinal direction marks "N", "E", "S", "W" in tiny serif caps at the four cardinal positions of the ring, with a sequence of fine hairline radial tick marks between each cardinal point (like a mariner's compass). The compass rose is line-only, no fill.

Color: brown ink only on cream card stock. NO TERRACOTTA, NO GOLD on this tier — these are reserved for higher ranks. The card stock is the same warm cream as the paper tone.

Mood: humble, fresh, "first day of the journey." This is the badge a user holds before they have done anything yet. It should look slightly understated next to the higher ranks.
```

## Tier II — Curious Cook (passport entry stamp)

```
Tier 2 of 5 — "Curious Cook." This is the first true ink-stamped badge. It belongs to the same visual family as a vintage European passport entry stamp.

Artifact: a circular ink stamp pressed onto warm cream paper. The outer ring of the stamp is a distressed brown ink circle with visible ink bleed, slight off-register patches, and small breaks in the ring where the rubber stamp didn't take ink fully. The badge fills roughly 90% of the frame.

Top arch: the Roman numeral "II" in small serif caps.
Center: the "NK" monogram in inked brown serif caps.
Bottom arch: "NIEVES' KITCHEN" in serif caps with bullet ornaments.

Ornamental ring: four small etching-style cooking objects orbit the monogram at the four cardinal positions of the ring — a wooden spoon at the top, an open recipe book at the right, a small sprig of herbs at the bottom, a small whisk at the left. Each object is rendered as a tight copperplate-etching line illustration, no shading wash, just disciplined linework. Between each pair of objects sits a small terracotta-colored dot ornament.

Extra detail: a small secondary dated postmark — a fragmentary circular ink impression with a partial date legible (e.g. "5 · MAY") — partially overlaps the lower-right edge of the main badge, as if a second stamp landed slightly on top of the first.

Color: brown ink dominant. Terracotta enters here for the first time but only as small accents — the dot ornaments between the objects, and a hint of terracotta on the secondary postmark. The bulk of the linework remains brown. No gold.

Mood: first real ink, official "you cooked something." It feels earned but humble.
```

## Tier III — Wanderer (layered postmark)

```
Tier 3 of 5 — "Wanderer." This is a busier, denser badge than Tier II. It is built from overlapping postal-cancellation impressions, as if a parcel has been routed through several offices.

Artifact: a circular badge made from layered postmark and cancellation marks pressed onto warm cream paper. The right edge of the circle is interrupted by a vertical row of small ticket-stub perforation holes — a single line of evenly spaced circular punches running top to bottom along that edge — as if the badge could be torn off a longer stub. The rest of the badge is otherwise round.

Top arch: the Roman numeral "III" in small serif caps.
Center: the "NK" monogram in inked brown serif caps.
Bottom arch: "NIEVES' KITCHEN" in serif caps with bullet ornaments.

Ornamental ring: a layered postmark composition. The ring contains:
- A set of three or four parallel wavy cancellation lines (the classic postmark cancellation pattern) running diagonally across one quadrant.
- Two smaller secondary dated postmark impressions overlapping each other, one in terracotta ink and one in brown, with partial dates ("12 · APR", "23 · JUL") legible inside them.
- Small fragments of vintage postal language at the edges of the ring: the words "PAR AVION" in tiny serif caps in one location, and a partial city-name ("LISBOA", "ISTANBUL") fragmenting off the edge.
- A thin route-line arc curving across one quadrant of the ring, with a small silhouette of a propeller airplane riding the arc (drawn in fine etching style).

Color: brown ink and terracotta in roughly equal weight — terracotta is no longer just an accent on this tier, it carries some of the secondary stamps and route-arc. Ink is denser overall, more layered, more "lived in" than Tier II. No gold.

Mood: well-traveled, slightly worn, layered history. The badge should look like it has been around.
```

## Tier IV — Globetrotter (wax seal on parchment)

```
Tier 4 of 5 — "Globetrotter." This badge is no longer an ink stamp — it is a physical wax seal pressed into a piece of parchment. The visual logic shifts from "printed" to "embossed."

Artifact: a round terracotta wax seal, approximately 80% of the frame width, sitting on a small piece of warm cream parchment. The wax seal has a scalloped, slightly irregular outer edge (as if pressed by a real seal stamp into hot wax) with one or two small drips at the bottom. The wax body is terracotta-colored with subtle warm-brown shadow tones suggesting raised-and-recessed embossing — every typographic and ornamental element on this badge appears RAISED out of the wax (lighter on the top edge of each shape, darker shadow underneath), not inked onto a surface. A small folded parchment ribbon trails below the seal — a short bifurcated tail like an old document seal.

Top arch: the Roman numeral "IV" raised/embossed in the wax. Not inked. The wax around the IV recedes slightly to give it dimension.
Center: the "NK" monogram raised/embossed in the wax. The most prominent raised element on the seal.
Bottom arch: "NIEVES' KITCHEN" in serif caps, raised/embossed in the wax.

Ornamental ring: a stylized globe forms the entire ring around the monogram. The globe is rendered with curved longitude and latitude lines forming a spherical grid. Two thin route arcs thread across the globe (one curving across the upper hemisphere, one across the lower), each arc punctuated by one or two tiny round pin dots marking destinations. Continents are NOT illustrated in literal detail — the globe reads as an abstract grid of meridians.

Color: terracotta wax body, warm brown shadow tones for embossing depth, no other colors. No gold on this tier yet — gold is exclusive to Tier V.

Mood: tactile, sealed, "officially noted." The badge should feel like a real wax seal you could touch — the embossing is the entire point of this tier.
```

## Tier V — Culinary Diplomat (gold-foil diplomatic crest)

```
Tier 5 of 5 — "Culinary Diplomat." This is the apex badge. It is a full diplomatic-credential medallion, the only badge in the set that uses gold.

Artifact: a round medallion approximately 90% of the frame width, presented as a full crest with multiple decorative elements layered over a warm cream paper background. The medallion has a clean circular outer border in fine brown ink. The treatment combines ink, terracotta, and vintage gold-leaf foil — gold is concentrated on the typography, the monogram, and small decorative highlights, while terracotta fills mid-weight ornamental areas and brown ink carries the line illustration.

Top arch: the Roman numeral "V" rendered in gold-leaf serif caps with subtle gold-foil texture (slight irregular flecks, slight metallic warmth — but still flat artwork, not 3D-rendered metal).
Center: the "NK" monogram, fully gold-leafed in elegant book-serif caps. This is the most ornate rendering of the monogram in the entire system. A fine gold hairline rule sits beneath the monogram.
Bottom arch: "NIEVES' KITCHEN" in gold-leaf serif caps with small gold bullet ornaments.

Ornamental ring (this tier is the densest — many decorative elements layer here):
- A laurel wreath wraps the entire monogram, made of two curving brown-ink olive branches meeting at the bottom and curving up around the monogram on either side, each branch dotted with small terracotta olives.
- Fine gold sun rays radiate outward from the center, extending beyond the laurel wreath and reaching toward the inner edge of the bottom arch — short sharp triangular rays alternating with even finer hairline rays.
- Four small five-pointed star ornaments in gold sit at the four cardinal positions of the ring, just inside the outer border.
- A horizontal ribbon banner in terracotta crosses the lower third of the medallion, passing IN FRONT OF the laurel base. The banner has subtle folds at each end (classic heraldic ribbon shape). The banner is LEFT BLANK — no text on the ribbon. Its role is purely visual ceremony.
- At the very base of the laurel wreath, tucked inside the lowest curve of the leaves, two small crossed utensils — a fork and a spoon — in fine brown-ink etching.

Color: brown ink for linework, terracotta for the ribbon and olives, gold-leaf accents for the typography, monogram, sun rays, and stars. The combination of all three colors (only on this tier) is what makes the badge read as the apex.

Mood: unmistakably official, ceremonial, "credential." This is the user's reward for completing a serious journey. It must read as a meaningful step up from the wax seal at Tier IV — denser, more decorated, and the gold is the visual signal that says "you have arrived."
```

Above each badge in small serif caps appears the Roman numeral I, II, III, IV, or V respectively; no other labels or text exist on the parchment outside the badges themselves.

Style anchors:
- Vintage letterpress and hand-drawn copperplate-etching aesthetic — early-20th-century European passport stamps and hand-set cookbook frontispieces. Slight ink bleed and distress at every stroke. No gradients, no drop shadows, no 3D rendering.

Badge 1 (leftmost): a round cream paper LUGGAGE TAG with reinforced metal eyelet at top, short twine loop, one torn paper edge. Brown ink only. Compass rose ornamental ring (N/E/S/W marks).

## Generation order (recommended)

1. Run the **lineup-sheet prompt** first. Iterate on it until the five-badge set reads as a coherent system. This catches "Tier IV looks too close to Tier III" or "the gold on V is too bright" issues cheaply.
2. Once the system feels right, run each **per-tier prompt** individually at 1024×1024 with transparent background for production quality. Iterate per badge.
3. Crop to a clean square, convert to WebP via `npm run optimize-images` (this repo's pre-commit hook will also auto-convert if the badges live under an opt-in directory listed in `.husky/pre-commit`), drop into `public/passport-tiers/`.
