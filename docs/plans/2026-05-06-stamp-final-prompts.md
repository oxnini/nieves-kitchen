## Boilerplate to paste after each prompt

### A. Anchor boilerplate — for Sections 1 (East Asia) and 10 (North America)

```
The stamp must fill the canvas — its outer border touches the canvas edges. No surrounding page, no paper margin, no canvas frame around the stamp. Render against a pure white background outside the stamp's border (no parchment, no cream, no wood, no any other background). Inside the stamp's border, the textural detail described (paper fiber, linen, cloth, plaster, bark, etc.) is desirable — this is the stamp's own surface, not the canvas.

no human faces, no portrait sculpture, no ceremonial mask faces, no anthropomorphic deity faces, no religious figure faces painted on architecture, no neon colors, no pure black, no fluorescent saturation, no stock-photo realism, no AI-cliché smooth gradient, no text errors, no garbled native script
```

### B. Ink-impression boilerplate — for Sections 2, 3a, 3b, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12, 13

```
The stamp must fill the canvas — its outer border touches the canvas edges. No surrounding page, no paper margin, no canvas frame around the stamp. Render against a pure white background both outside the stamp's silhouette AND inside the frame between ink lines — this white will be removed to transparent in post on both sides. The interior of the frame must not be a card, paper, cream wash, halo, or any opaque shape behind the motifs; it is the same canvas white as the outside, so that only the ink strokes survive when the white is keyed out. No substrate texture inside the silhouette. The result must read as a stamp pressed onto paper, not a sticker.

no human faces, no portraits, no masks, no deity faces, no religious figure faces, no neon colors, no pure black, no fluorescent saturation, no stock-photo realism, no AI-cliché smooth gradient, no text errors, no garbled native script, no cloth substrate, no fabric weave fill, no card fill inside the silhouette, no paper card inside the frame, no cream wash inside the silhouette, no opaque substrate behind the motifs, no die-cut card
```

### C. Cabinet etching boilerplate — for Sections 11a (Mesoamerica) and 11b (Caribbean)

```
The engraved illustration must fill the canvas — its outermost ink strokes touch the canvas edges. No surrounding page, no paper margin, no canvas frame around the illustration. There is no outer cartouche, no rectangular or oval or scalloped border, no ribbon perimeter — the engraved illustration itself defines the stamp's extents; only the inked strokes are opaque. Render against a pure white background both outside the illustration AND in every pocket of negative space between cross-hatching strokes — this white will be removed to transparent in post on both sides, leaving only the single-ink line work. No substrate texture, no card fill, no cream wash, no parchment halo behind the strokes; no flat colour blocks. The result must read as a single-ink engraved stamp pressed onto paper, not a sticker.

no human faces, no portrait sculpture, no ceremonial mask faces, no anthropomorphic deity faces, no religious figure faces in the engraving, no neon colors, no pure black, no fluorescent saturation, no stock-photo realism, no AI-cliché smooth gradient, no flat color blocks, no text errors, no garbled native script, no outer cartouche, no rectangular border, no oval border, no scalloped border, no ribbon perimeter, no off-register double-strike, no second-ink accent, no two-tone palette, no card fill behind the engraving, no parchment background, no cream background, no opaque substrate behind the lines, no sticker look, no die-cut card, no woven cloth substrate, no carved stone substrate, no glaze
```
